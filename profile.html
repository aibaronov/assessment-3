My name is Arthur Baronov

I'm currently a student at Devmountain. While pursuing my PhD in applied mathematics, I began studying programming because I felt this would pair well with math. However, I discovered that there were many things that I enjoyed more about programming than about solving math problems all day. After completing my first semester in the PhD program, I decided that I would rather pursue software engineering as a career for several reasons:

  • I believe software development uses more logic. Math professors don't want to admit this but there's a lot of memorization required in math.
  • I love being able to create an actual product or a tool that people can use to solve problems.
  • In most cases, math can't be applied to a real world problem unless it's used in software.
  • I enjoy hanging out with / talking to programmers.
  • The programming community is more supportive of one another.
  • There are way more job opportunities!
